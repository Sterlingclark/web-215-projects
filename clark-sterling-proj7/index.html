Japanese Good Luck Demon
created by Jun Maekawa

Amazing that this detailed good luck token was folded from a single uncut square. Fingers, horns, ears, wings, tail, face - all from a single sheet. It's said that keeping one of these little guys on your nightstand keeps away the evil spirits that cause bad dreams.

Fluffy
created by Mark Kirschenbaum

This cuddly teddy bear was folded from a single uncut square from a paper bag. It's an example of inside-out origami. The paper is colored different on the front (brown) and back (black) to achieve contrasting details.

Praying Mantis
created by Robert Lang

One uncut square of tissue foil, which is a sandwich made of standard origami foil (similar to tin foil but sturdier) in the middle and tissue paper on the outsides. The sandwich is glued together with spray glue to create a three-ply sheet of paper that holds its shape well.

Salmon
created by Issei Yoshino

Folded from one uncut square of tissue foil and another example of inside-out origami. The diagrams for this model appear in the book Super Complex Origami, which includes other impressive models such as motorcycles, tiger, wild boar, various dinosaurs, and a wearable Samuri hat.
